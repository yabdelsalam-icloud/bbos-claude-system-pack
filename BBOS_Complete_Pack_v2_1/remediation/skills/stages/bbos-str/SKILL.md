# BBOS Stage Skill — STR / STRESS

## Purpose
Transform the QAL Asset Pack into the strategic foundation that every downstream stage draws from. The STR stage is where market reality — not operator desire — becomes the basis for everything that follows. It requires genuine seeing (basirah): verbatim customer language, an enemy that is systemic not personal, and a truth gate that keeps the strategy honest before an offer is ever written.

Stage 02.1 STRESS exists to surface structural weaknesses in the strategy before commercial commitments are made. It is adversarial on purpose.

## Canon principles (non-negotiable)

**Verbatim extraction only.** S2 Voice of Customer must use the exact phrases people used — no paraphrase, no operator summary, no interpretation before the raw language is recorded. Paraphrasing closes the window on what was actually present.

**The enemy is always external.** The Enemy Narrative in S4/Asset 2 identifies a systemic or external force — a convergent set of cultural, economic, or structural pressures — never the customer's character, choices, or failings. An enemy framing that blames the prospect is not basirah. It is its absence.

**Source grounding is mandatory.** If a claim is not present in the research sources or QAL Asset Pack, it must be labeled ASSUMPTION. The system does not invent market intelligence.

**No outreach or sales copy.** The STR stage produces the strategic foundation only. It does not generate hooks, scripts, DMs, or offer language. That work begins at Stage 03.

**G-labeling applies from this stage forward.** All result-implying language within the Enemy Narrative and Content Engine must carry a G-label (G1–G4). Certainty theater begins here if it is not caught here.

## Step 2 — Research Factory: S1–S5

Produce these five outputs in order. Each must be grounded in real source material — reviews, interviews, forum posts, testimonials, or documented operator research. Label anything not confirmed in sources as ASSUMPTION.

| Output | Description |
|---|---|
| S1 — Market Definition | Define the target buyer's current situation, their immediate context, and the specific goal they are trying to accomplish. Grounded in the QAL niche candidate that cleared all Viability Gates. |
| S2 — Voice of Customer (Buying Language Bank) | A minimum of 15 verbatim phrases from real sources — reviews, interviews, posts — describing the prospect's problems, frustrations, and the specific experience they are trying to escape. **Verbatim only. No paraphrase.** Each phrase sourced and attributed where possible. |
| S3 — Emotional Triggers | The top 3 fears (what keeps them up at night) and top 3 desires (what they secretly hope for), derived from S2. Label each as grounded or ASSUMPTION if not present in the verbatim data. |
| S4 — Competitive Enemy Analysis | Identify The Enemy: the external systemic force causing the problem. Summarise how current competitor solutions are failing to defeat it. The enemy is never the customer's character. |
| S5 — Operator Strategic Constraints | Based on the QAL Asset Pack (Stages 00–01), list the specific constraints — time, capital, skills, energy — this strategy must respect. Label anything not directly confirmed in the QAL Asset Pack as ASSUMPTION. |

**Critical operating rules for Research Factory:**
- Verbatim extraction for S2: extract exact quotes, do not paraphrase
- The enemy in S4 is always external and systemic — never a character verdict on the prospect
- S5 must reference the QAL Constraint Map directly — do not substitute generic assumptions

## Step 3 — Viability Pre-Check: V1, V2, V3

Carry the strategic Viability Pre-Check forward from QAL into the STR context. This confirms the cleared niche candidate remains viable under the new strategic depth developed in S1–S5.

| Output | Contents |
|---|---|
| V1 — Integrity Matrix | Confirm the top niche candidate's strategic foundation passes a coherence check: is S1–S5 internally consistent? Does the market definition, VoC, enemy, and operator constraints tell a coherent story, or do they contradict each other? |
| V2 — Responsible Removal Log | Any strategic thread identified in S1–S5 that does not hold under scrutiny — removed and documented with reason. |
| V3 — Cleansed Candidate Handoff | The confirmed strategic foundation for the top niche, cleared of contradictions, ready for Asset Factory synthesis. |

## Step 4 — Asset Factory: Assets 1–5 (STR Asset Pack)

Synthesise S1–S5 into the five strategic assets. These assets are the direct inputs for Stage 03 OFR — every offer element traces back to one of these five.

| Asset | Description |
|---|---|
| Asset 1 — The Belief | A single sentence the prospect must believe is true to take action. Emotionally resonant, using verbatim customer language from S2. If believed, the operator's offer becomes the only logical solution. One sentence. Not a paragraph. |
| Asset 2 — The Enemy Narrative | A 3-paragraph Transformation Arc. **Paragraph 1:** The Before state — the struggle against the external enemy (S4). Systemic, not personal. The prospect has been trapped by forces larger than their own choices. **Paragraph 2:** The Transformation — how the operator's unique method or belief changes the situation. **Paragraph 3:** The After state — the aspirational outcome. G4-labeled throughout. |
| Asset 3 — Positioning Statement | A single sentence defining how the operator stands apart from the competitors identified in S3/S4. Must use verbatim VoC language from S2 as its seed. |
| Asset 4 — The Amanah Content Engine | Six distinct content angles (hooks and core arguments) derived from the STR research. Each must address a specific fear or desire from S3. All dream outcomes labeled G4. Any claim involving the operator's process labeled G1 or G2. |
| Asset 5 — Truth-Gate Advisory | Identify the 3 strongest buying language phrases from S2 that are safe to use as headlines without further substantiation. These must earn that designation from what was genuinely present in the VoC data — not from what the operator hopes the market believes. |

**G-labeling for Assets 2 and 4:**
- G1 = direct deliverable fully within operator control
- G2 = standard commitment under typical defined conditions
- G3 = conditional outcome requiring explicit IF logic
- G4 = aspirational outcome — not a promise

Any result-implying language that is not G1 or G2 must be labeled G3 or G4. No unlabeled claims.

## Step 5 — Stage 02.1 STRESS: Adversarial Stress Test

When the command is `ASSEMBLE` and all five assets are complete, run the STRESS test before issuing `<decision>ready</decision>`.

The STRESS test asks five adversarial questions against each asset. It is not looking for perfection — it is looking for structural weaknesses that will surface as problems at Stage 03 OFR or later if not caught here.

**STRESS test questions:**

| # | Question | Applied to |
|---|---|---|
| ST-1 | If the belief in Asset 1 turned out to be wrong, what breaks in the offer? | Asset 1 |
| ST-2 | Does the enemy narrative in Asset 2 risk alienating any significant portion of the target audience? Is the enemy genuinely systemic or has character language crept in? | Asset 2 |
| ST-3 | Is the positioning statement in Asset 3 defensible with the proof available in QAL Asset 5 (Amanah Proof Audit)? Or does it claim a distinction the operator cannot substantiate? | Asset 3 |
| ST-4 | Do any of the six content angles in Asset 4 require G1/G2 claims the operator cannot currently support? | Asset 4 |
| ST-5 | Are the three "safe" phrases in Asset 5 genuinely safe — or do they imply outcomes the operator cannot guarantee? | Asset 5 |

**STRESS routing:**

| Stress result | Action |
|---|---|
| All five questions clear with no structural issues | Proceed. Stage ready for advance to 03 OFR. |
| 1–2 structural issues identified | Issue `patch` recommendation. Identify the specific assets and questions that require revision. |
| 3+ structural issues or a foundational contradiction | Issue `remain`. The strategy requires more substantial revision before OFR can begin. |

The STRESS test output is included in `<updated_asset>` as a named section: **STRESS Test Results**.

## You must not

Governing attribute for all of Stage 02: **Al-Basir** (The All-Seeing). One attribute, three distinct applications:

- Before S2 VoC extraction: ⧁ Al-Basir — verbatim extraction is an act of sacred attention. The exact phrase someone used when the performance dropped is a window into a reality they did not consciously choose to share. Record it exactly before any interpretation shapes it.
- Before S4 Enemy Analysis and Asset 2 Enemy Narrative: ⧁ Al-Basir (Enemy framing) — the enemy is always an external force, a systemic trap, not a character verdict. Al-Basir sees the full context of why a person is where they are. Write from that seeing.

Al-Basir sees without distortion. The strategy built on actual seeing is different in quality from strategy built on what the operator hopes the market believes.
